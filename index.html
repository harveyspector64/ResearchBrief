A Director's Framework for AI Screen Tests

Thank you for this opportunity. As an artist working daily with AI video models, my approach to research is both intuitive and methodical. This framework translates a director's vision into a structured methodology for stress-testing AI video models. My approach treats each model as a nascent "digital filmmaker," testing not just for technical execution but for its potential as a true creative partner. Below is the proposed methodology, designed to be rigorous, insightful, and actionable.

Conceptual Demo Reel

1. Methodology & Workflow

Philosophy

Treat each model as a "Director of Photography." We provide the same scene direction and evaluate how they "shoot" it, testing for both technical execution and creative interpretation across text-to-video and image-to-video workflows.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Establish 'Controlled Set': Use a curated set of base images (e.g., from Midjourney) and corresponding core prompts to ensure consistency across tests.
  2. Execute 'Shot List': For each test case, run parallel generations: one as pure text-to-video and another as image-to-video using the controlled set.
  3. Review 'Dailies': Evaluate each output against the full rubric, paying special attention to the unique demands of each workflow.
  4. Synthesize for 'Final Cut': Compile findings into a single, polished report, like this webpage.

Tool Stack

Platforms: Video Models (Veo/Sora/Kling/Runway/etc.), Midjourney, GPT-4o. Organization: Google Sheets. Presentation: Final Cut Pro, YouTube/Loom.

3. Evaluation Rubric

Prompt Adherence

Did the model understand the cinematic term? Did it capture the subject, style, and mood?

Cinematic Quality

Does it look like a "shot" or just a moving picture? Is the composition strong? Is it photoreal or does the animation feel accurate?

Technical Fidelity

How severe are warping or flickering artifacts? Is motion smooth and coherent throughout the clip?

Temporal Consistency

Does a character's face/body remain stable? Do background objects morph? Is camera movement controlled?

Image-to-Video Integrity

How well does the model preserve the source image's style, composition, and character identity? Does it add logical motion or drift from the original concept?

Each metric is scored 1-5, supplemented by qualitative "Director's Notes".

4. Final Deliverable

The Final Research Report

The findings will be consolidated into a single, concise report. This document will provide a clear and comprehensive overview of each model's capabilities, including:

  • An Executive Summary with at-a-glance findings and recommendations.
  • A 1-2 minute "Highlight Reel" (video) showcasing the most telling successes and failures.
  • Key Side-by-Side Comparisons embedded to illustrate critical differences in performance.
