Conceptual Demo Reel

1. Methodology & Workflow

Philosophy

My approach is to test each model for both technical execution and creative potential across text-to-video and image-to-video workflows, providing the same core direction to each to ensure a fair comparison.

Beyond controlled tests, a crucial part of the evaluation involves iterative prompting. After establishing a baseline, I would probe each model’s flexibility with prompt alterations to discover creative workarounds. This reveals not just what a model can do under ideal conditions, but how to achieve the best results in real-world use.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Establish Controlled Set: Use a curated set of base images and corresponding core prompts to ensure consistency across tests.
  2. Execute Test Cases: For each test case, run parallel generations—one as pure text-to-video and another as image-to-video.
  3. Analyze Generations: Evaluate each output against the full rubric, paying special attention to the unique demands of each workflow.
  4. Synthesize Findings: Compile all data and qualitative notes into a single, polished report, like this website, with tables, graphs, or example embeddings.

Tool Stack

Platforms: Video Models (Veo/Sora/Kling/Runway/etc.), Midjourney, GPT-4o. Organization: Google Sheets. Presentation: Website, Final Cut Pro, YouTube/Loom.

3. Evaluation Rubric

Poor (1-2) Adequate (2.1-3.9) Excellent (4-5)

Prompt Adherence

Did the model understand the prompt's intent? Did it capture the subject, style, and mood?

Visual & Aesthetic Quality

Does the output have strong visual appeal? Is the composition effective? Does it successfully achieve the intended aesthetic?

Technical Fidelity

How severe are warping or flickering artifacts? Is motion smooth and coherent throughout the clip?

Temporal Consistency

Does a character's face/body remain stable? Do background objects morph? Is camera movement controlled?

Image-to-Video Integrity

How well does the model preserve the source image's style, composition, and character identity? Does it add logical motion or drift from the original concept?

Each metric is scored 1-5, supplemented by qualitative "Expert Notes." For the full analysis, scores can be aggregated and visualized in a heatmap to provide an at-a-glance comparison of each model's core competencies.

4. Final Deliverable

The Final Research Report

The findings will be consolidated into a single, concise report. This document will provide a clear and comprehensive overview of each model's capabilities, including:

  • An Executive Summary with at-a-glance findings and recommendations.
  • A 1-2 minute "Highlight Reel" (video) showcasing the most telling successes and failures.
  • Key Side-by-Side Comparisons embedded to illustrate critical differences in performance.

Case Study: A Practical Workflow Example

To illustrate my analytical process, here is a practical case study comparing two models on a specific task (a tracking shot with an image prompt) and demonstrating a hybrid solution. The prompt was: "Tracking shot of a man walking through an ancient city at night, shot on 35mm film with natural lighting."

1. Model A (Hailou)

Result: Successfully captures the camera motion but has lower visual fidelity and some glitching.

2. Model B (Sora)

Result: Higher visual fidelity but fails to execute the tracking shot, losing coherence and creating undesirable motion and edits.

3. Hybrid Solution

Result: Hailou's output is remixed in Sora, combining the correct motion with superior visual quality. This takes advantage of Sora's strengths and Hailuo's lower costs.

This example demonstrates that the "best" model is context-dependent, and that deep knowledge allows for hybrid workflows that leverage the unique strengths of each tool to achieve a desired result.
